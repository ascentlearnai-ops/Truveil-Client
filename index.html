PRIVACY PLEDGE

What Truveil Secure will and won't do.

Full transparency. Nothing happens without your consent.

What we DO

  • Verify your session code — confirm the interview exists and is active.
  • Monitor focused window — know when you switch apps during the call.
  • Capture interview audio — only after your explicit consent prompt.
  • Report integrity signals — pass anomalies to your recruiter's dashboard.
  • Close automatically — the moment your recruiter ends the session.

What we DON'T

  • Read your files — Truveil Secure never touches your documents or folders.
  • Capture your screen — we never record or screenshot your display.
  • Log keystrokes — no keylogging, ever. We only see interview audio.
  • Persist after close — nothing remains when the window closes. Portable and forgettable.
  • Touch your browser — zero extensions, zero browser access, zero cookies.

FAQ

Answers, upfront.

Is this spyware?

No. Truveil Secure only runs when you open it, only monitors things directly related to the live interview (focused window, interview audio, session state), and closes itself when the recruiter ends the session. Nothing runs in the background. Nothing persists. Uninstall is "delete the file."

Do I need to install it?

Yes, but it is lightweight and does not need admin permissions. If your IT org blocks unknown installers, check with your recruiter for a signed fallback.

What if I don't have the session code?

Your recruiter will email or message you a 10-character code starting with TRV-. If you don't see it, check your spam folder, then ask the recruiter to resend.

Will it slow down my computer?

No. Truveil Secure uses under 1% CPU during an average interview. It's lighter than a Chrome tab.

What if my interview is on Zoom / Meet / Teams?

It works with all three. Truveil Secure doesn't replace your video call — it runs alongside it. Just join your interview normally in Zoom, Meet, or Teams, and keep Truveil Secure open while you talk.

Can I close it mid-interview?

You can — but your recruiter will see a "session interrupted" flag on their side, which usually isn't what you want. The app is designed to be there for the duration of your call and close itself when the interview wraps.
